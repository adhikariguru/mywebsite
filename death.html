THE DEATH

Darkness creeps upon me,

Shrouding my soul in pain,

Memories of life now fleeting,

As death approaches like a stain.

My body aches with torment,

Trauma etched upon my face,

My heart beats weakly,

As I prepare to leave this place.

The light fades from my eyes,

As I lay here on my bed,

The gothic shadows dance around me,

As I wish for death to bring me rest.

My breath is shallow,

My thoughts consumed by fear,

The end is near,

And I cannot escape this tear.

But in the darkness,

I find a glimmer of hope,

For in death,

I will find true peace and cope.

My pain will be no more,

My trauma left behind,

I will be free,

And death, I will not mind.
