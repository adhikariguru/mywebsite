Nepal's Political Crisis Triggers Financial Downturn

Written by Manish Adhikari
Date: 8th March, 2023



The current political crisis in Nepal has unleashed a plethora of challenges for the country's financial market, leading to a severe downturn. The recent betrayal of the coalition by Prime Minister Pushpa Kamal Dahal with CPN UML has further intensified the ongoing political turmoil, resulting in a loss of confidence among investors. The repercussions of this political imbroglio have resulted in the ouster of the taxpayers' favourite Finance Minister, causing significant disruption in the financial market. Consequently, investors have lost a substantial amount of capital from their portfolios, and the stock market has experienced a sharp decline, contributing to the prevailing sense of uncertainty in the financial market.

The unstable political climate in Nepal has significantly impacted the financial market, leading to several challenges. The lack of confidence in the government has triggered a sell-off by both domestic and foreign investors, further exacerbating the bearish sentiment. As a result, the stock market is currently undergoing a severe downturn, exacerbating the sense of uncertainty and instability in the financial market.

The Nepal Rastra Bank (NRB) has been instrumental in the country's financial market's regulatory functions, particularly in regulating inflation. To this end, the NRB has implemented several measures, including reverse repo and increasing collateral requirements. However, these measures have inadvertently led to the draining of banking and market liquidity, creating an unfavourable environment for the banking and financial sector. The resulting tight liquidity conditions have added to the existing challenges of the financial market.

The bearish sentiment pervading the Nepalese financial market has had ripple effects across several sectors, significantly impacting the overall economy. Reduced consumer spending, resulting from the prevailing bearish sentiment, has had a negative impact on the country's GDP. The ongoing political crisis has further complicated the Nepalese economy's challenges, which is already grappling with high unemployment and a burgeoning debt burden.

In conclusion, the ongoing political crisis in Nepal has severely impacted the country's financial market, leading to a sharp decline. The NRB's efforts to regulate inflation have resulted in further challenges, including the tightening of liquidity conditions. The bearish sentiment in the financial market has had a ripple effect on the overall economy, necessitating swift government intervention to restore stability in the financial market and avoid further economic downturn.
